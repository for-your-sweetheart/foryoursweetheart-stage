Fight Diabetes,
One Heartbeat
at a time

Over 400,000 people in Singapore are living with diabetes[1]. Yet, many Singaporeans with diabetes don’t know about their increased risk for heart disease[2].

Read on to understand the link between diabetes and your heart.

 

Over time, uncontrolled high blood sugar can damage blood vessels and nerves leading to the heart[3].

This can ultimately raise blood pressure levels[3].

Having diabetes can also increase the level of bad cholesterol and decrease the level of good cholesterol[4].

All these factors increase the risk for heart disease in diabetic patients.

Diabetic patients are 2 to 4 times more likely to develop heart disease[3].

About 1 in 2 heart attack victims in Singapore have diabetes[1].

References: [1]Ministry of Health. The War on Diabetes. Last accessed June 2018. | [2] Boehringer Ingelheim data on file | [3]World Heart Federation. Cardiovascular Disease Risk Factors. Last accessed May 2018. | [4]American Heart Association. Cholesterol Abnormalities and Diabetes. Last accessed June 2018.
